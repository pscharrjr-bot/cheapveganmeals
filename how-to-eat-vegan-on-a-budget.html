How to Eat Vegan on a Budget

Cheap vegan eating gets much easier when you build meals around inexpensive staples and repeat the combinations that work.

Start with cheap staples

Rice, oats, pasta, potatoes, beans, lentils, peanut butter, and frozen vegetables can cover a surprising amount of ground. These kinds of ingredients make it easier to build meals that are both affordable and filling.

Keep the system simple

People often make cheap eating harder by chasing too much variety too fast. Repeating a few breakfasts, lunches, and dinners that actually work for your life can lower cost and decision fatigue at the same time.

Think in meal patterns

Bowls, soups, wraps, pasta, and oatmeal are all low-cost meal formats that can be changed with small ingredient swaps. When you think in patterns instead of isolated recipes, cheap vegan eating becomes easier to sustain.
