Budget Pasta Salad

A cheap lunch idea that keeps well in the fridge and works especially well for meal prep.

Budget Pasta Salad

Prep time: 15 minutes - Cook time: 10 minutes - Total time: 25 minutes
Estimated total cost: $8.90 - Estimated cost per serving: $2.23 - Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces pasta - $1.20
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas or white beans, drained - $0.80
  • 1 cucumber, chopped - $0.80
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes - $1.20
  • 1/2 red onion or small regular onion, chopped finely - $0.40
  • 2 tablespoons oil - $0.20
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar or lemon juice - $0.20
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder - $0.05
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt - $0.05
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper - $0.05
  • Optional: chopped parsley or olives - $0.50

Detailed Instructions

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta until just tender.
  2. Drain the pasta and rinse it under cool water so it stops cooking.
  3. While the pasta cools, chop the cucumber, tomatoes, and onion.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the oil, vinegar or lemon juice, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Add the cooled pasta to the bowl.
  6. Add the beans and chopped vegetables.
  7. Toss everything together until evenly coated.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  9. Chill for at least 20 minutes before serving if you want the flavors to blend more.

Helpful Notes

  • This gets better after sitting in the fridge for a little while.
  • Add extra beans if you want it more filling.
  • Use any cheap pasta shape you already have.
